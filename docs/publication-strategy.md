# EduPlay Academic Publication Strategy

**Title Concept**: "Adaptive Hybrid Mastery Systems in Multi-Tenant School Environments"  
**Authors**: EduPlay Research Team, Principal Data Scientist

## Objective
Establish absolute domain authority by peer-reviewing EduPlay's Item Response Theory + LLM Generation logic in pure academic rigor. Prove to school boards that we are not "just another gamified app."

## Sections

### 1. Methodology
- **Fusing BKT with IRT**: Detail the mathematical foundation. How EduPlay utilizes Bayesian Knowledge Tracing to infer $P(L_t)$ (probability of learning mastery at time $t$) alongside classic IRT logit formulas to calculate absolute Ability $(\theta)$.
- **Dynamic Difficulty Adjustment (Elo system)**: How pacing is maintained exclusively within the Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD).

### 2. Statistical Validation
- Compare the impact engine logs against standard linear worksheets.
- Show standard deviation reduction and acceleration of time-to-mastery in $n=5,000$ simulated cohorts.

### 3. Adversarial Resilience
- Document our AST (Abstract Syntax Tree) sanitization strategies.
- Explain our determinism validation (Phase 2): How we prove that LLM-generated math problems have exactly one provable answer before displaying them to students, eliminating AI hallucination risk entirely.

### 4. Deployment Architecture
- Highlight the real-world engineering constraints: Neon PgBouncer scaling, Vercel Edge compute limitations, and distributed Redis event architectures for multi-tenant school isolation.

### 5. Ethical AI Controls
- Data minimization and "Right to be Forgotten" implementation.
- Overcoming AI bias in generated content.
- Hard AI Cost limits ensuring equitable bandwidth allocation per school.
